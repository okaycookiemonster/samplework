GUTTER BALL ALLEY

INT. BOWLING ALLEY - DAY
The fluorescent lights hum over a surprisingly clean bowling alley. J (34), N (35), S (36), and W (40) are engaged in a fierce, if entirely one-sided, battle against the pins. 
PLAYER | SCORE
---|---
J      | 12
N      | 8
S      | 15
W      | 10
J winds up and releases the ball. It rolls a perfect, agonizingly slow straight line… veering directly into the left gutter. The screen lights up: “GUTTER”.
J
(Gasps, hysterically; hands on the head)
Oh, no! My little bowling baby! It just wanted to explore the side paths! It’s an adventurer!
N
(Taking a sip of his beer)
Yeah, an adventurer into the trench. That’s like, your fifth one, J. You’re practically a gutter ball magnet.
J
It’s not my fault! This lane has a personality! It’s clearly a rebel. It’s doesn’t want to be constrained by the rules of the game! It wants to harmonise; to create an adventure!
S steps up. He performs a series of ballerina-like movements, then delivers the ball. It veers sharply left.
S
(Eyes wide with disbelief)
IMPOSSIBLE! I calibrated my stride! I factored in the humidity! This is a statistical anomaly! This lane is… defective!
W
(Calmly, adjusting his headband)
Or maybe you just threw a gutter, S. It happens. Like, a lot, to us.
W bowls. The ball wobbles, then veers right.
W
(Smile dimming)
Ah, yes. The spirit of the gutter. It calls to me. Furguson would be so disappointed. He always said, "W, embrace the pins, not the void."
N
Alright, alright, suckers. Watch and learn from the guru. This is how you don't hit the gutter.
N casually tosses it down the lane. It takes a sudden, inexplicable swerve to the right.
N
(Stares at the gutter)
What the hell was that? Did the ball just… betray me? I swear, I felt a tremor. A tiny, unholy tremor.
S returns with a comically oversized hot dog.
N
Yeah. His stomach betrayed him again. See the audacity.
S
This. This is my solace. A perfect union of smoked meat and artisanal bread. No disappointment of the gutters can touch the pure joy contained within this tube of perfection.
He takes a bite. A glistening bead of relish plummets directly onto his pristine shirt. The hot dog topples out of the bun.
S
(Horrified whisper)
No. No, no, no! My shirt! The Hot Dog! The mustard of doom! This is a stain on my honour!
J
Oh, S! My little relish baby! It just wanted to explore your shirt! It’s just an adventurer!
S
Don’t you dare purilieze my hot dog disaster, J! A culinary betrayal!
J
See! It’s the lane! It’s a bad omen. We need to perform an exorcism!
N
That’s like saying the gutter has feelings.
S
There are no "gutter spirits," J. There is only our collective, inexplicable incompetence!
W
(Begins singing "The Winner Takes It All")
Maybe the gutter is just misunderstood. Maybe it’s lonely.
J
I’ve got it! We’re trying to avoid the pins! It’s reverse psychology bowling!
W
When you smile like that, I get the Heebie-Jeebies. Stop It!
S
J. That is… the most profoundly idiotic idea I have ever heard. And yet… it’s so perfectly us.
The monitor lights up: “GUTTER”.
[SCENE ENDS]
